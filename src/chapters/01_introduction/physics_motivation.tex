\section{Physics Motivation}

\subsection{Lepton Flavor Universality}

The Standard Model (SM) organizes the charged leptons into three generations:
the electron, muon, and tau. Although these particles have different masses,
their electroweak gauge interactions are otherwise identical. This statement is
known as lepton flavor universality (LFU). In the charged-current weak
interaction, LFU implies that the $W$ boson couples to each charged lepton
flavor with the same strength,
\begin{equation}
  g_e = g_\mu = g_\tau,
  \label{eq:lfu-couplings}
\end{equation}
up to kinematic effects induced by the different lepton masses.

One test of LFU is provided by the charged-pion branching-ratio
measurement
\begin{equation}
  R_{e/\mu}
  =
  \frac{
    \Gamma\!\left(\pi^{+}\to e^{+}\nu_e(\gamma)\right)
  }{
    \Gamma\!\left(\pi^{+}\to \mu^{+}\nu_\mu(\gamma)\right)
  }.
  \label{eq:remu-definition}
\end{equation}
The notation $(\gamma)$ indicates that radiative photons are included in both
the experimental and theoretical definitions of the observable. Since both decay
modes originate from the same parent particle, many common hadronic
uncertainties cancel in the ratio. In particular, the effects of 
the pion’s internal structure cancel at first order in the ratio, leaving it primarily 
sensitive to the charged-lepton masses and the weak interaction.
\cite{pioneer_rare_2022,pioneer_proposal_2022}.

At tree level,
\begin{equation}
  R_{e/\mu}^{(0)}
  =
  \left(\frac{g_e}{g_\mu}\right)^2
  \frac{m_e^2}{m_\mu^2}
  \left(
    \frac{
      m_\pi^2-m_e^2
    }{
      m_\pi^2-m_\mu^2
    }
  \right)^2.
  \label{eq:remu-tree}
\end{equation}
Assuming LFU, the coupling ratio becomes unity. The remaining suppression of
the electronic decay mode arises from the charged-lepton mass dependence of the
weak interaction.

The strong suppression of the electronic decay channel is a consequence of the
spin and chiral structure of the weak interaction. The charged pion is a
spin-zero pseudoscalar meson, while the charged-current weak interaction couples
to left-chiral neutrinos and right-chiral antileptons. In the pion rest frame,
angular-momentum conservation requires the outgoing antilepton to occupy a
helicity state disfavored by the weak interaction. The decay can still occur
because the charged antilepton is not massless, but the amplitude becomes
proportional to the charged-lepton mass.

At tree level, charged-pion decay proceeds through a virtual \(W\) boson, as
shown in \figref{fig:pion-decay-feynman}. The matrix element may be written
schematically as
\begin{align}
\mathcal{M}_{fi}
&\propto
\underbrace{
\left[
\frac{g_W}{\sqrt{2}}
f_\pi
p_\pi^\alpha
\right]
}_{\text{pion vertex}}
\left[
\frac{g_{\alpha\beta}}{m_W^2}
\right]
\underbrace{
\left[
\frac{g_W}{\sqrt{2}}
\bar{u}(p_\ell)\gamma^\beta
P_L
v(p_\nu)
\right]
}_{\text{lepton vertex}} .
\label{eq:pion-matrix-element-intro}
\end{align}

The detailed spinor algebra is given in
Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-tree-level-remu}, but the important result is that
the chiral structure of the weak interaction forces the amplitude to scale with
the charged-lepton mass,
\begin{align}
\mathcal{M}_{fi}
&\propto
\frac{g_W^2 f_\pi}{m_W^2}
m_\ell
\left(m_\pi^2-m_\ell^2\right)^{1/2}.
\label{eq:pion-helicity-suppression}
\end{align}
Consequently,
\begin{align}
|\mathcal{M}_{fi}|^2
&\propto
\left(
\frac{g_W}{m_W}
\right)^4
f_\pi^2
m_\ell^2
\left(m_\pi^2-m_\ell^2\right).
\end{align}
Including the two-body phase-space factor gives
\begin{align}
\Gamma(\pi\to\ell\nu_\ell)
&\propto
m_\ell^2
\left(m_\pi^2-m_\ell^2\right)^2 .
\label{eq:pion-rate-scaling}
\end{align}

The factor of \(m_\ell^2\) strongly suppresses the electronic decay mode,
overwhelming the larger phase space available to the electron channel. As a
result, the muon mode dominates charged-pion decay even though the electron mode
is kinematically favored.

\subsection{\texorpdfstring{$R_{e/\mu}$ as a Precision Probe of New Physics}{Re/mu as a Precision Probe of New Physics}}

The same helicity suppression that makes $\pi^{+}\to e^{+}\nu_e$ rare also
makes $R_{e/\mu}$ highly sensitive to small non-SM contributions. Interactions
with different chiral structure, such as scalar or pseudoscalar charged-current
operators, need not inherit the same helicity suppression as the SM amplitude.
As a result, even small beyond-SM contributions can produce observable shifts
in the electronic decay channel.

The SM prediction for the ratio is
\begin{equation}
  R_{e/\mu}^{\mathrm{SM}}
  =
  1.23524(15)\times10^{-4},
  \label{eq:remu-sm-value}
\end{equation}
with a relative uncertainty at the $10^{-4}$ level
\cite{cirigliano_rosell_2007,pioneer_rare_2022}. The current experimental world
average is
\begin{equation}
  R_{e/\mu}^{\mathrm{exp}}
  =
  1.2327(23)\times10^{-4},
  \label{eq:remu-exp-value}
\end{equation}
consistent with the SM prediction but less precise by approximately a factor of
15 \cite{pienu_2015,pioneer_rare_2022}.

Possible deviations from LFU can be parameterized through flavor-dependent
charged-current couplings,
\begin{equation}
  g_e \neq g_\mu .
\end{equation}
As shown in Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-tree-level-remu}, the tree-level pion
decay rate scales as
\begin{equation}
  \Gamma(\pi\to\ell\nu_\ell)
  \propto
  g_\ell^2
  m_\ell^2
  \left(m_\pi^2-m_\ell^2\right)^2 .
\end{equation}
At tree level, the ratio of the electronic and muonic decay channels gives
\begin{equation}
  R_{e/\mu}
  =
  \left(
    \frac{g_e}{g_\mu}
  \right)^2
  \left[
    \frac{
      m_e(m_\pi^2-m_e^2)
    }{
      m_\mu(m_\pi^2-m_\mu^2)
    }
  \right]^2 .
\end{equation}
A fractional deviation in the coupling ratio therefore produces approximately
twice the fractional deviation in the measured branching-ratio ratio.

The current comparison between experiment and theory corresponds to
\begin{equation}
  \left(\frac{g_\mu}{g_e}\right)_{R_{e/\mu}}
  =
  1.0010(9),
\end{equation}
consistent with LFU
\cite{pioneer_proposal_2022}.

PIONEER Phase~I aims to improve the precision of this test by approximately an
order of magnitude, substantially increasing the sensitivity to possible
deviations from lepton flavor universality and other non-SM charged-current
interactions.
